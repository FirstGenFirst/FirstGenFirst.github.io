About Us

FGF was founded by Jazmin Reyes in July 2020 as an undergraduate at Stanford University. Throughout her childhood, Jazmin saw her family and her community suffer through poverty, racism, and an unjust education system as a first-generation and low-income student herself. Our goal is to help other FGLI students in similar situations get better access to higher education. From the very early stages of daydreaming about college to getting accepted, FGF aims to ensure that no FGLI student is ever alone.

Our Team

Jazmin Reyes – Founder

Hello! My name is Jazmin Reyes and I founded FGF in hopes to support first-generation and low-income students like myself through scholarships and sufficient resources. My vision is to lead more students into college by providing helpful insights that I wished someone had told me and tips I have learned through my own experiences in high school and college.

Caroline Tran – Design

Hi! I'm Caroline Tran and I am an undergraduate at Stanford University from Seattle, WA. As a first-generation college student, I've been diving head first into every new experience, so I know how it feels to be overwhlemed and lost. I joined FGF in hopes of helping other first-gen students navigate the high school to college transition while using my passion for mentorship and art.

Alan Gallardo – Marketing

Hey guys! I'm Alan and I am a current undergraduate at UCLA studying Communications and Film.
